Katie Gray

Project Manager

Architect with 10+ years experience spanning commercial interiors in D.C. to project management for development and design firms in Nashville. Originally drawn to historic preservation, found strength in streamlining complex projects.

Quick Win: 45 approvals in just six months, with one project turned around (from intake to plan approval) in only 23 hours!
What is one thing you do that makes a huge difference in your approval outcomes?

I personally call the reviewers and AHJs and speak to them in a polite and friendly manner. When it comes down to it, they are people doing their jobs just like us, and they deserve to be treated as such. Adding the human element and decency to say "thank you" goes a long way.

What is one thing Pulley does better (or differently) from traditional expediting?

It is so much more organized and transparent than traditional expediting services. With Pulley, not only do you have a platform that provides real-time information and tracking, but the Project Managers are absolutely dedicated to getting your permit across the finish line.

Is there a jurisdiction that presented a challenge? How did you tackle it?

I was brought onto a project in Tampa, FL where the building permit had holds that halted issuance. Tampa requires Water and Wastewater Commitment Letters - a 5-6 week process. Pulley got an Early Start Notice from the AHJ so the GC wasn't held up. I called the Water Department weekly until we received approvals 3 weeks ahead of schedule.
